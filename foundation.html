What we are committed to

Specific, concrete, accountable. Not vague CSR language.

✉️

Personal letter from Cayde to every displaced worker. When an Aria business replaces a staff role, the person who held that role gets a personal letter from our founder — not a form email, not an automated message — with real pathways forward and an offer to talk.

💰

1% of all Aria revenue funds the Foundation. From day one. Not when we "get to scale". Not when investors allow it. 1% goes to the Foundation every month, used for retraining programs and community support in regional Australia.

🎓

Aria Academy — free retraining platform. Currently in development. When it launches, every displaced worker affected by Aria automation gets 6 months of free access to learn skills for the new economy — AI management, digital operations, business ownership.

🤝

First access to Aria roles as we grow. As the Aria team expands, people who have been displaced by our technology get first consideration for any positions we open. We build the team we displaced from.

📊

Annual public impact report. Every year we publish exactly how much went to the Foundation, what it funded, and how many workers it helped. No vague claims. Actual numbers.

What we have not solved yet

Honest acknowledgment of where we fall short.

Aria Academy is not live yet

The most significant thing we can do — provide free retraining — is not ready yet. We are building it. Until then, personal letters and direct conversations are what we have. It's not enough. We know that.

1% is a small number

At early revenue levels, 1% funds almost nothing. As Aria grows, this becomes meaningful. We are committing to the percentage from day one so the discipline is baked in before it's easy to change.

We cannot reach every displaced worker

We can only reach the people whose roles our customers tell us about. If a business replaces a role without telling us, we do not know who to contact. We are working on making this part of our standard offboarding process.

We have not solved the systemic problem

The Aria Foundation is one company's response to one piece of a much larger problem. AI displacement is a society-wide challenge. We are not pretending we can fix all of it. We are fixing the part that is directly our responsibility.

If you lost your job to AI

Whether it was Aria or another AI product — if automation affected your livelihood, you deserve real support. Not sympathy. Real options.
