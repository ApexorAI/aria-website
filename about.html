Aria did not start in a boardroom. It started with a question.

Why are the tools that large corporations use to run their businesses completely out of reach for the small business owner on your street?

We watched business owners lose customers every day to missed calls, slow follow-ups, and admin that never ended. Not because they did not care. Because they were doing everything themselves.

"The technology to fix this existed. It just cost $50,000 a year in staff."

So we built Aria.

But as we built it we kept coming back to the same uncomfortable truth. This technology replaces jobs. Real jobs. Held by real people in real communities.

We are not a corporate team optimising for shareholder returns. We are from regional Victoria. We know these communities. We know what it means when someone loses their livelihood.

So we made a decision. If we are going to build the thing that changes the workforce — and we believe it is going to happen whether we build it or not — then we are going to be the ones who give a damn about what happens to the people affected.

"That is why every business that joins Aria and replaces a staff role gets a personal letter from our founder offering that person a pathway forward."

That is why we are building the Aria Foundation. 1% of every dollar we make goes back into retraining the workers and communities that AI automation affects in regional Australia.

We are not pretending this is not happening. We are building the thing and taking responsibility for it.

That is Aria.

Mission

To give every Australian small business access to enterprise-level AI capabilities — and to do it responsibly.

That means making powerful tools affordable. It means telling the truth about what AI does to jobs. And it means building infrastructure to support the people affected.

🔓

Accessibility

The tradie in Ballarat deserves the same tools as the corporation in Sydney. We are building to close that gap permanently.

⚖️

Accountability

We list every job we replace publicly. We write to every displaced worker personally. We fund retraining with 1% of revenue. No hiding.

🤝

Authenticity

We say what we actually think. We do not use corporate language to hide uncomfortable truths. We are from this community and we act like it.

What we believe

→

AI automation is inevitable. It will happen with or without Aria. We would rather build it with accountability than leave it to people who do not care about the consequences.

→

Small business is the backbone of Australia. There are 2.5 million small businesses in this country. Most of them are being left behind by technology that should be helping them.

→

Regional communities matter. We are based in Ballarat. We know what happens to communities when work disappears. That is why we are building something to replace it, not just remove it.

Founding philosophy

"Live by the sword, die by the sword."

We built the thing that displaces workers. So we live with that. We are accountable to it. And if the world ever displaces Aria the same way — we will accept that too.

We built Aria knowing exactly what it does. It replaces jobs. Real jobs. Held by real people who need them to live.

We built it anyway — because we genuinely believe it will be better for Australian small businesses and for Australian communities if this technology is accessible to everyone, not just companies that can afford enterprise software.

But we are not going to pretend the displacement doesn't happen. We are not going to hide behind "the future of work" and "new opportunities" while real people lose real income.

So we made rules for ourselves.

01

We list every job we replace publicly. No euphemisms. Every role, every salary, every AI employee that takes it. You can see it all on the jobs page.

02

Every displaced worker gets a personal letter from Cayde. Not a form email. A real letter with real options for what to do next.

03

1% of all revenue goes to the Aria Foundation. Funded retraining, scholarships, and community support for people displaced by AI automation in regional Australia.

04

We never optimise for growth at the expense of accountability. If we ever catch ourselves hiding something uncomfortable, that is a signal we have lost our way.

We are not the good guys. We are the honest guys. There is a difference.

The good guys do not build AI that replaces receptionists. The honest guys build it, list every job it takes, write letters to every person affected, and fund the programs to help them find what's next.

That is what it means to live by the sword.
